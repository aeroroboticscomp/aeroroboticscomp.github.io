Frequently Asked Questions:

2019-2020 Competition Specific Questions

Autonomous portion

  • How will the (CSV) “waypoint file” be provided to the Team? (on paper?, USB thumb drive, CD, email, other?) please specify.

    It will be delivered through USB thumb drive

  • How many (number?) waypoints may be expected for the flight path? (the origin, start/return waypoint may be stated as a single waypoint)?

    5 total waypoints: one delivery waypoint, 3 loiter, and 1 home waypoint

  • What type of surface will the golf ball be delivered? Hard (like concrete/tile) or rough (like a grassy field)?

    It will be on grass

  • Will this task be performed inside or outside??

    It will outside since there is no GPS signal inside

Semi-autonomous portion

  • Can you provide the dimensions of the “Competition Field”?

    Diameter of the competition area (dotted circle in diagram)?

    100 ft diameter

  • Shortest distance from the competition area (dotted circle in diagram) to the center tennis ball (# 3 of 5) on the ground?

    ~25 feet

  • Approximate (perpendicular) distance from the “Home Base” (center) to the center-line of (nearest) 5 tennis balls on the ground?

    Home base will be 10 feet away from the tennis balls.

  • Distance from the “Home Base” (center) to the center-line of the ball drop “hoops”?

    35 feet.

  • Spacing of the 5 starting tennis balls (on the ground)?

    6-7 inches.

  • Will the 5 tennis balls be arranged as depicted in the diagram of the competition rules?

    Yes

  • Are the tennis balls sitting on the ground, or a tennis ball holder? (If tennis ball holder, please describe)

    Cardboard flat holders, drone will have full 180 degree access

  • Height of the ball drop hoops from the ground?

    Between 2 and 4 feet.

  • Distance from the center point of the competition area to each hoop? Please specify each hoop (L, M, S) and the distance & direction from the competition area center point.

    10 feet-S, 10 feet-M, 20 feet-L

  • Inside diameter of each of the 3 hoops?

    12”, 16” and 20”

  • If a Team drops a tennis ball and misses a hoop, may the ball be retrieved for another attempt to drop it through a hoop (within the allotted time)? …or only one attempt per tennis ball?

    Correct. A ball may be retrieved if it “missed the hoop”.

  • Rules state, “During flight, intentional collisions/interference are prohibited. ​Teams causing a collision will be disqualified.” What is that Team disqualified from? The entire Semi-Autonomous portion of the competition?

    The entire competition.

  • Does this mean that UNintentional collisions/interference is allowed (or forgiven)? …and will not disqualify a Team?

    Correct. We will have judges to deem whether collisions were intentional or not. If a team is exhibiting any behavior of wreckless flying, we will deem that as “intentional”. However, if a collision is unintentional it will be forgiven and the judges have the right to provide another round for those two teams to compete.

  • If an opposing Team causes a “collision” compromising the performance or function (task/time) of our UAV during competition, will our Team be able to repair the damage and re-compete (i.e. do-over) the task?

    Yes. A second flight will be allowed. That second flight will be a “single flight” – i.e. there will not be another drone in the competition field.

  • Will this task be performed inside or outside?

    Outside. However, in the event there is inclement weather this will occur inside the gymnasium.

Presentation

  • Will there be common resources for use by any Team during presentations? Such as easels, small table, (computer) video projector/screen, sound equipment, Wi-Fi, etc. Or will the Team be responsible for all resources as listed?

    The team will only be responsible for submitting their presentation 7 days in advance of the competition to aero.robotics.comp@gmail.com. ARC will provide all other needed presentation supplies.

  • Will the presentation be conducted inside a room/facility (not outside)? (CA only)

    We have a classroom reserved at Littlerock for the presentations. I have attached a tentative schedule for the day as well.

  • Is each presentation open (for observation) to other people, who are not part of the presenting Team and Judges? (CA only)

    ARC allows observers. Teams are allowed to choose who they want in the room. I.e. a team has the right to tell observers to leave the room.
